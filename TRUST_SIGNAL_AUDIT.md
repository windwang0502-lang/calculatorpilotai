## Trust Signal Evaluation

### Strengths
1. **Privacy-first messaging** — "Your data never leaves your device" appears in content
2. **Free messaging** — Repeated emphasis on "100% Free", "no hidden fees"
3. **AI transparency** — Separate AI Disclosure page explaining AI usage
4. **Editorial standards** — Editorial Policy page builds content trust
5. **Contact expectations** — Clear response time reduces uncertainty

### Opportunities for Enhancement
1. **Update frequency** — "Updated regularly" could be more specific
2. **Accuracy claims** — "Industry-standard formulas" could reference specific sources
3. **Professional verification** — Could mention expert review more prominently

## Recommendations

### Medium Priority
1. **Add "Verified by experts"** badge where applicable (BMI, Mortgage)
2. **Add "Built for US users"** to homepage trust badges (already present, keep)

### Low Priority

2. **Add "Results reviewed by professionals"** to disclaimers

## What NOT To Add (Overkill)

- Trust badges on every page
- "Official" or "Certified" claims
- Fake user testimonials
- Excessive security badges
- Star ratings without real data

**The current trust signals are sufficient and feel authentic.**

## Conclusion

CalcWise AI has appropriate, non-spammy trust signals that:
- Are contextually relevant
- Don't overpromise
- Build credibility authentically
- Focus on real value propositions (free, privacy, accuracy)
